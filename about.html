About This Project

The Eaton Fire Experience

This interactive web experience was created to document and memorialize the significant impact of the Eaton Fire on Altadena and the surrounding Los Angeles communities. By combining data visualization, immersive storytelling, and personal accounts, we aim to educate the public about wildfire dynamics while honoring those affected by this devastating event.

Our goal is to transform complex data into an emotionally resonant experience that helps visitors understand not only what happened during the fire, but also the human stories behind the statistics. We hope this project serves as both a memorial and an educational resource that contributes to community resilience and wildfire awareness.

MIT Reality Hack: LA Fires

MIT REALITY HACK LOGO

This project was conceptualized and developed during the MIT Reality Hack hackathon, specifically at the Los Angeles satellite location. The hackathon brought together developers, designers, and storytellers to create mixed reality experiences that address real-world challenges.

Our team chose to focus on the Los Angeles wildfires, recognizing the profound impact these events have on local communities and the need for innovative approaches to disaster documentation, education, and memorialization. The project evolved from concept to prototype during an intensive development sprint, with continued refinement afterward to create the experience you see today.

Methodology & Data Sources

The information presented in this experience was compiled from multiple authoritative sources, including official reports from the Los Angeles Fire Department, CAL FIRE, and the National Weather Service. Satellite imagery and progression data were obtained from NASA Earth Observatory and NOAA.

Personal testimonies were collected through interviews with residents, first responders, and community leaders, with permission granted for their inclusion in this memorial. All participants were provided the opportunity to review their contributions before publication.

For a comprehensive list of data sources and reference materials used in the creation of this experience, please visit our Resources page.
